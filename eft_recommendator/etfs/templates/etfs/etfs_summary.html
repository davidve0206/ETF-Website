How do you interpret this graph?

For some context, Volatility is how much an investment's returns vary from their average. In summary, High volatility means big price swings, while Low volatility means steadier prices with less risk.

As such, points in the Top-Right of the graph are high-risk, high-reward ETFs, more suited for investors that can afford to lose some or even most of their money if their investment goes south, while points in the Bottom-Left are low-risk, low-reward ETFs, more suited for investors who just want to keep their savings safe but want higher returns on their savings than your average bank account.
